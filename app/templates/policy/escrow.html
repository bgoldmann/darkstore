Escrow & Dispute Policy

This store uses 2-of-3 multisig escrow. The platform never holds a single key: buyer, seller, and the market (mediator) each hold one key. Funds move only when two of three sign (happy path: buyer + seller; dispute: mediator + the party we rule for). See Monero multisignature and our research doc for details.

Time limits

  • Auto-finalize: Escrow auto-releases to the seller after a set window (e.g. 14–30 days) unless a dispute is opened.
  • Disputes can only be opened before the auto-finalize time. Extensions may be granted by support where appropriate.

How to open a dispute

  • Before the auto-finalize deadline, go to the order page and use "Open dispute".
  • PGP is required: You must have a public key set on your account. Send evidence (e.g. proof of shipment/delivery, terms) encrypted to the platform key. Evidence is handled via PGP; do not paste sensitive data in plain text.
  • The mediator (support/admin) will review and decide: release to seller or release to buyer.

Mediator role

Support and admin can: mark escrow as funded (when payment is confirmed), resolve disputes by choosing "Release to seller" or "Release to buyer". In 2-of-3 multisig terms, the mediator signs with the party they rule for. Mediators do not have access to move funds alone.
